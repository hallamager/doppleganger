WELCOME TO

DOPPLEGANGER PRODUCTIONS

Doppelganger Productions is a not-for-profit theatre company based in Bournemouth.

  • Students

    Our strong ties with Bournemouth University and Arts University Bournemouth has led to collaborative projects with student groups. It is our aim to forge more creative pathways where programmes of student playwriting can be performed under the auspices of Doppelganger Productions.

  • playwrighting

    We specifically produce innovative new writing and stage new work by emerging and established playwrights with a focus on Dorset-based writers. We aim to create a vibrant theatre culture in Bournemouth and to present our work to audiences nationally and internationally.

  • workshops

    As part of our commitment to new writing we run Doppelganger Dialogues, a free bi- monthly theatre-writing workshop which nurtures innovative writing and offers support to emerging playwrights. Selected projects originated in Doppelganger Dialogues are developed through rehearsed readings and workshops to produce full-length works.

ABOUT DOPPLEGANGER PRODUCTIONS

Based in Bournemouth, we are the resident theatre company of The Winchester Arts Pub which is a fringe theatre venue. We have performed at the Allesbrook Theatre of Bournemouth University, the Lighthouse Poole, the Shelley Theatre Bournemouth, Scaplen’s Court Poole, Chaplin’s Cellar Bar Boscombe, The Bread and Roses Theatre Clapham, Regent’s College London and the Charing Cross Theatre London. We have been staging plays in the Bournemouth Arts by the Sea Festival since its year of inception in 2011 and in Bournemouth University’s Festival of Learning since 2013.

    John Foster

    Artistic Director

    John Foster teaches writing at the Media School of Bournemouth University and the London School of Film, Media & Performance at Regent’s University, London.

    He has published fiction in the Guardian and Spectator, broadcast talks for radio, and worked as a script editor, researcher and associate producer in television.

    His credits include episodes for television series such as Z-Cars, Softly Softly, Crown Court, Emmerdale, Juliet Bravo, Rockliffe, Saracen, The Bill. He has written childrens’ serials, single dramas and dramatised documentaries, including a BAFTA award- winning BBC Omnibus on crime novelist Raymond Chandler.

    The movie of his screenplay Letters from a Killer featured Patrick Swayze in the lead and he is contributor to the award-winning crime fiction collection, Mean Time.

    He has beeninvolved in the creation of multi-media dance dramas and his work has been staged at the Edinburgh Fringe. A co-founder of Doppelganger Productions, he is currently engaged in writing for theatre.

    Ted Street

    Executive Producer

    Ted Street has a BA (Hons) in English and Media Studies. He has wide ­ranging experience in corporate communications as a freelance writer, producer and director working on a diverse range of subjects for national and international companies.

    He works as a freelance audio­visual technician and has expertise in sound recording and recording studio installation. He also manages production of the Helios Type 69 range of professional audio mic pre/EQ electronics originally designed by the late Richard Swettenham of Olympic Studios.

    He has worked in Higher Education giving technical support to media production undergraduates on video location drama and documentary projects, and on educational drama programmes for the Consortium for Drama in Higher Education.

    He is co­-founder of Doppleganger Productions, Tour Manager for Surya Arts Theatre ­in Education and a volunteer drama worker with Action on Addiction.

    Richard Broadbent

    Coordinator of Doppleganger Dialogues

    Richard is a mature student currently studying scriptwriting at Bournemouth University.

    He has written a number of promising screenplays and published two short story collections.

    He also enjoys writing: poetry, music and lyrics for his band ‘The Friendly Natives’.

    He is interested in new writing of all kinds as well as discussing how to develop works to a production stage, whether that be: publishing books, putting on theatre productions or making films.

    He hopes that the Doppelganger Dialogues will continue to provide a platform for writers to get constructive feedback for their work in order to go further with it in a positive direction as well as acting as a valuable network of support and information from fellow writers and artists.

    Charmaine K Parkin

    Associate Theatre Practitioner

    Charmaine trained under the theatre practitioner and teacher Brian Astbury on the pioneering Acting and Contemporary Theatre course at the East 15 Acting School.

    After graduating she worked intensively as an actor under her maiden and stage name Charmaine Szecowka, performing at the Edinburgh Fringe Festival and touring in Germany and France with White Horse Theatre.

    Notable roles included Eliza Doolittle and Juliet in Shakespeare’s Romeo & Juliet, which was performed on the balcony in Verona for Teatro Stabile.

    In 2011 she created bθk Theatre in Bournemouth with the aim of using the powerful tool of theatre to benefit everyone.

    She has written and directed many shows, which have toured schools and events across the South of England; one of which was performed at the Slade’s Farm Olympic Torch Relay Celebration in Bournemouth and another on Brownsea Island.

    Charmaine is a co-founder of SISATA, that brings to life classic plays adapted for four actors to produce energetic, fun and playful pieces of theatre that are suitable for all ages.

    Now a visiting director for White Horse Theatre in Germany, she has a huge passion for Shakespeare and making theatre as inclusive, interactive and accessible as possible. She directed MACBETH 2070, for Dramatic Productions at Poole Lighthouse, a modern adaptation transporting the infamous tale into a sci-fi world with the purpose of engaging a younger audience.

    Dedicated to helping produce new writing in the theatre and creating an alternative theatre scene in Bournemouth and beyond she is also an active member of Doppelganger Productions and has previously directed, The Murder Wife and The Picture of Dorian Gray.

    Simon Jay

    London Management

    Simon spent seven years developing as a theatre-maker and writer, first at university and then through various companies producing plays for charity such as The Vagina Monologues (V-DAY 2011), Queer Macbeth (Terrance Higgins Trust) and A Night Against Putin (Amnesty International).

    Simon directed a triptych of plays for Black History Month as well as a string of Shakespeare adaptations: Hamlet (2010) The Tempest (2012) and Romeo & Juliet: A Plunderphonica (2014). He has also worked with The Young Vic Director’s Network, The Soho Theatre Young Company and The National Theatre of Wales on research and development projects. Simon writes regular reviews and features for Theatre Performance Guide and Guru, having been a staff writer since 2013.

    This year, Simon has written the screenplay for the award-winning LGBT filmmakers Looking at You Productions; the film is called Heaven’s To Betsy and will star Lorraine Chase. In March Simon published his first book, Bastardography, which is memoir about growing up gay and mentally ill, but with a limitless passion to be creative: as an E-Book, it has been an Amazon UK Number 1 bestseller and will be published in paperback later in the year.

    Most recently Simon directed Jennifer’s Robot Arm in collaboration with Dawn of The Replicants’ Paul Vickers. His second play for Carabosse, Four Walls, was shown at The Buzby in Milton Keynes in May 2015.

    Roger Emmott

    Strategy Adviser

    Roger Emmott is a professional writer, business developer, innovator and adviser to firms at all stages of their growth.

    He is Doppelganger Production’s Strategy Adviser, where draws on over 30 years of highly varied international experience working with companies of all sizes and shapes. His experience includes planning and running events of many types.

    He holds a Masters Degree in Screenwriting from Bournemouth University in addition to degrees from the University of Birmingham, and London Business School.

    He is a Chartered Engineer and Fellow of a number of professional institutes. He is a keen advocate of fringe theatre and live music. He is also an avid photographer. His firm Roger Emmott Associates Limited helps industrial businesses to learn, perform and grow. It has strong expertise in the metals and mining value chain.
